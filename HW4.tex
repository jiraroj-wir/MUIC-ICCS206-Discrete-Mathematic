\section*{Problem 2}
You bring 5 cola yoyos and 6 grape yoyos for 10 students. Students pick two yoyos at random:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*.]
    \item Two grapes: eat one grape, return the other.
    \item Two colas: eat one cola, return the other.
    \item One of each: eat the grape, return the cola.
\end{enumerate}
The last yoyo in the bag goes to you. Which flavor can you guarantee for yourself, and why?

\section*{Problem 3}
Start with the numbers $1,2,3,4,5,6$. Repeatedly pick two numbers $(a,b)$, remove them, and insert $|a-b|$. Show that the final remaining number is always odd.

\section*{Problem 4}
A circle is divided into six sectors labeled $1,0,1,0,0,0$. You may pick two neighboring sectors and increase both numbers by 1. Can you make all six numbers equal?

\section*{Problem 5}
On an $8 \times 8$ chessboard with standard coloring you may flip the colors of any entire row or column (all squares in that row/column). Can you reach a configuration with exactly one black square?

\section*{Problem 6}
An island has 13 blue, 15 red, and 17 green pokemons. When two different-colored pokemons meet, they both transform into the third color (e.g., blue + red $\rightarrow$ two greens). Show that it is impossible to reach a state with only one color remaining. (Hint: consider counts modulo 3.)
